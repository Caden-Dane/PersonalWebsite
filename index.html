Books That Shaped Me

Rich Dad Poor Dad

Rich Dad Poor Dad

by Robert Kiyosaki

Contrasts two perspectives on money—one from a financially struggling "poor dad" and one from a wealthy, financially savvy "rich dad"—to highlight how financial education, asset-building, and mindset shape wealth and success.

Atomic Habits

Atomic Habits

by James Clear

Revolutionary insights on building good habits and breaking bad ones. This book changed how I approach personal and professional growth.

The Richest Man in Babylon

The Richest Man in Babylon

by George Samuel Clason

Shares financial wisdom through parables set in ancient Babylon, emphasizing principles like saving consistently, living below your means, investing wisely, and seeking knowledgeable advice.

How to Win Friends and Influence People

How to Win Friends and Influence People

by Dale Carnegie

Teaches practical strategies for building strong relationships, gaining trust, and influencing others through genuine interest, active listening, appreciation, and respectful communication.

A Trusted Advisor

A Trusted Advisor

by Robert M. Galford

Explores how professionals can build deep client relationships by earning trust through credibility, reliability, empathy, and a focus on the client’s interests over their own.

Mindset

Mindset

by Carol S. Dweck

The power of believing you can improve. This book shaped my approach to learning and facing challenges in tech.

Moonwalking with Einstein

Moonwalking with Einstein

by Joshua Foer

Follows journalist Joshua Foer’s journey into the world of competitive memory, revealing how ancient mnemonic techniques can dramatically improve memory and cognitive performance.

User Stories Applied

User Stories Applied

by Mike Cohn

Explains how to write effective user stories in agile development, showing how they enhance communication, guide development, and ensure software delivers real value to users.
